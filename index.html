ABOUT

ICT (Information and Communications Technology) refers to all technologies that are used to handle information and support communication. It includes the use of computers, the internet, software, networks, and digital devices that allow people to create, store, process, share, and manage information efficiently. In simple terms, ICT is what powers the modern digital world we live in today—from sending messages on social media to attending online classes, and even running businesses online.

BTLED

The Bachelor of Technology and Livelihood Education (BTLED) is a teacher education program that prepares students to become effective educators in Technology and Livelihood Education (TLE). It focuses on developing both theoretical knowledge and practical skills in areas such as home economics, industrial arts, entrepreneurship, and information and communication technology. The program aims to produce competent and innovative teachers who can deliver hands-on and meaningful learning experiences that help students gain essential livelihood skills for employment, personal development, and future success.

BACHELOR OF TECHNOLOGY AND LIVELIHOOD EDUCATION

BTLED is a 4-year college degree program in teacher education focused on Technology and Livelihood Education (TLE) in basic education. It prepares students to become licensed teachers who can teach practical, skills-based subjects in schools.

ENSEMBLES OF LIVELIHOOD TECHNOLOGY STUDENTS
